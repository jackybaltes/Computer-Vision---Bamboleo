Bamboleo

Bamboleo is a popular skill game

Playing field consists of a yellow disk and several game pieces (e.g., cubes, prisms, cones)

Pieces are placed on yellow disk and then the entire disk is balanced on top of a cork ball

Players take pieces off the yellow disk one at a time. Loose 4 points if you topple the board

Project

Students from Ostfalia Uni. in Germany and NTNU in Taiwan will join to implement a Sawyer robot playing this game against humans or other robots

Sawyer robot is a co-bot from Rethink Robotics

It features a single compliant robot arm, a camera, a face tablet, and a camera in the gripper of the robot

Playing Strategy

The board is perfectly balanced if and only if the center of mass of all the pieces is above the pivot point

If the center of mass is not above, then it will create a torque at the pivot point, which will rotate the board

Torque is counteracted by rotated around the corkball

If friction of the pieces on the board is less than drag of gravity, then the pieces will start to slide and the board will topple

Strategy

Move center of mass towards the pivot point

Only move allowed is to remove a pieces

So, we must remove the pieces so that the center of mass of the remaining pieces is as close as possible to the pivot point

Greedy strategy is sufficient: Always remove the pieces which results in the center of mass being as close as possible to the pivot point

Human Strategies (Mentioned in the Video)

Pick a piece from the side tilted down

Pick pieces that are close the center/pivot point

These make sense if we consider the center of mass of the system

Center of Mass

Center of Mass of a set of particles (point masses) is the point that if we apply a force, we can move the entire set of particles without them tilting

As a kid you will have experimented with the center of mass if you tried to balance a mobile phone on your finger

The center of mass is the point where there is no resulting torque from the point masses, that is, the sum of the moments of the point masses is 0 at the center of mass

Moment: A mass \(m\) at position \(x\) generates a torque at the pivot point \(P\) that is equal to the mass \(m\) multiplied by the distance between the pivot point \(p\) and the position \(x\) of the mass \(m\)

Center of Mass

Center of Mass: Point where the sum of all the moments of all particles \( (p_1, p_2, p_n) \) is 0

\[ \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i * ( x_{com} - x_{i} ) = 0 \]

Center of Mass

Solve for \( x_{com} \)

\[ \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i * ( x_{com} - x_{i} ) = 0 \\ \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i * x_{com} - \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i x_{i} = 0 \\ x_{com} \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i - \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i x_{i} = 0 \\ \]

Equation for CoM: \[ x_{com} = \frac{ \sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i x_{i} }{\sum_{i=1}^{n} m_i } \] 

def calc_com( pieces ):
  mx = np.sum( [ p.weight * p.pos for p in pieces ] )
  m = np.sum( [ p.weight for p in pieces ] )  
  return mx/m
